Code of Conduct and Rules & Regulations — KR Mangalam University

1. General Code of Conduct
   KR Mangalam University expects all students, faculty, and staff to maintain the highest standards of professional ethics, integrity, and respect. The Code of Conduct Committee oversees compliance with institutional policies.

2. Student Code of Conduct
   a) Attendance Requirements:
      - Students must maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in each course to be eligible for end-semester examinations.
      - Students falling below the attendance threshold will receive a warning after the mid-term exam period.
      - Attendance shortage may result in debarment from end-semester examinations.
      - Medical leave must be supported by valid medical certificates submitted within 7 days of rejoining.

   b) Academic Integrity:
      - Plagiarism, cheating, or any form of academic dishonesty is strictly prohibited.
      - Use of unauthorized materials during examinations will result in disciplinary action.
      - Students found guilty of academic dishonesty may face grade cancellation, suspension, or rustication.
      - All submitted work must be original unless properly cited.

   c) Campus Behavior:
      - Students must carry their university ID card at all times on campus.
      - Smoking, consumption of alcohol, and use of any banned substances on campus is strictly prohibited.
      - Students must not damage or misuse university property.
      - Use of mobile phones during lectures and exams is prohibited unless permitted by the faculty.
      - Students must adhere to the dress code specified by their respective schools.

   d) Examination Rules:
      - Students must reach the examination hall at least 15 minutes before the scheduled time.
      - No student will be allowed to enter the examination hall 30 minutes after the exam begins.
      - Students must not carry any electronic device, notes, or unauthorized material into the examination hall.
      - Any misconduct during exams will be reported to the Unfair Means Committee.

3. Faculty and Mentor Guidelines
   a) Teaching Responsibilities:
      - Faculty must maintain punctuality and complete the syllabus within the prescribed timeline.
      - Faculty must upload lecture notes, assignments, and other resources on the university ERP system.
      - Faculty must take regular attendance and update it on the ERP.
      - Faculty should be available during office hours for student consultation.

   b) Mentorship Program:
      - Each faculty member is assigned a group of students as a mentor.
      - Mentors are responsible for guiding students on academic performance, career planning, and personal development.
      - Mentors must meet their assigned students at least once every two weeks.
      - Mentors should identify students at risk of academic failure and provide timely intervention.

   c) Research and Ethics:
      - Faculty must follow ethical research practices as per UGC guidelines.
      - Research involving human subjects must obtain Ethics Committee approval.
      - Faculty are encouraged to publish research in reputed journals and involve students in research projects.

4. Disciplinary Actions
   Depending on the severity of the offense, the following actions may be taken:
   - Verbal warning and counseling
   - Written warning with a copy to parents/guardians
   - Suspension from classes for 1 to 4 weeks
   - Withholding of results or examination privileges
   - Debarment from university events and activities
   - Rustication from the university
   - Filing of FIR with local authorities for criminal offenses

5. Grievance Redressal Mechanism
   - Students can file grievances through the university's online portal.
   - The Internal Complaints Committee handles issues related to harassment and discrimination.
   - An Ombudsperson is available for independent resolution of disputes.
   - All complaints are handled confidentially and resolved within 15 working days.
   - Grievance Redressal page: krmangalam.edu.in/iqac/grievance-redressal-mechanism

6. Dress Code
   - Students must dress in a manner that is decent, professional, and appropriate for an academic environment.
   - Specific dress codes may apply for laboratory sessions, workshops, and practical classes.
   - Students appearing in formal university events must follow the prescribed formal dress code.

7. Library Rules
   - Students must maintain silence in the library at all times.
   - Books must be returned within the prescribed loan period (14 days for general, 7 days for reference).
   - Loss or damage of library books will be penalized.
   - Students must not deface, mark, or tear library materials.

8. IT Usage Policy
   - University IT resources must be used for educational and research purposes only.
   - Unauthorized access to university systems, hacking, or spreading malware is a punishable offense.
   - Students must not use university Wi-Fi for downloading illegal content or accessing prohibited websites.
