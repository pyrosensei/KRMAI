Student Welfare Services — KR Mangalam University

1. Dean of Student Welfare (DSW)
   Dean: Dr. Shweta Bansal
   Assistant Dean: Dr. Shivani Sehgal

   Vision: Comprehensive individual, academic, and integrated development by providing life skills and counseling services for diverse, global societies.

   Mission: To facilitate global leadership and personal well-being through high moral values and sound life skills among students.

2. Core Functions of DSW
   a) Student Council & Engagement:
      - Supervises student activities and governance.
      - Promotes extracurricular involvement in sports, cultural events, and academic competitions.
      - Ensures active student participation in university decision-making through the Student Council.

   b) Welfare & Facilities:
      - Manages hostel operations, canteen services, and campus infrastructure for students.
      - Addresses student grievances through dedicated channels.
      - Coordinates with school coordinators for day-to-day student support.

   c) Financial Support:
      - Administers scholarship programs (merit, category, entrance-exam based).
      - Manages fee concession and installment requests.
      - Identifies students in financial need and provides assistance.

3. Counseling & Mentorship
   - The university provides unbiased guidance from experienced thought leaders.
   - Faculty mentoring relationships strengthen bonds and help students identify aspirations.
   - Self-paced learning support and career pathway selection based on individual skills and interests.
   - Dedicated counseling cell for mental health and emotional support.
   - Sessions on stress management, time management, and interpersonal skills.

4. Grievance Redressal Mechanism
   - Comprehensive system to address grievances from students, faculty, and staff.
   - Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) handles harassment-related complaints.
   - Ombudsperson available for independent dispute resolution.
   - Online grievance submission portal available at krmangalam.edu.in/iqac/grievance-redressal-mechanism
   - All complaints are treated confidentially.

5. Student Organizations
   - 14+ clubs and societies facilitate skill development, talent showcase, and experiential learning.
   - Domains include: cultural, academic, creative, technology, sports, environment, and community service.
   - Students can join clubs during the registration period at the start of each semester.
   - Clubs are supervised by faculty conveners and co-conveners.

6. Anti-Ragging Oversight
   - The DSW oversees anti-ragging measures across campus.
   - Dedicated Anti-Ragging Committee investigates all complaints within 7 working days.
   - National Anti-Ragging Helpline: 1800-180-5522 (24/7 toll-free).
   - Mandatory anti-ragging affidavit for all students and parents at admission.

7. Health & Medical Support
   - On-campus medical facility for first aid and minor ailments.
   - Partnerships with hospitals including Medanta for health camps and emergency services.
   - Regular health awareness programs and biochemical testing sessions.
