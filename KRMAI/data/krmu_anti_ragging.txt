Anti-Ragging Policy — KR Mangalam University
Zero Tolerance Policy (UGC/AICTE Compliant)

1. Definition of Ragging
   Ragging includes any act that causes physical or psychological harm, fear, or embarrassment to a student. This includes but is not limited to:
   - Forcing a student to perform tasks against their will
   - Verbal abuse, bullying, or intimidation
   - Any form of physical violence or threat of violence
   - Forcing juniors to address seniors in a particular or demeaning way
   - Any act that affects the mental health or dignity of a student
   - Cyberbullying, online harassment, or defamation on social media
   - Any act of discrimination based on caste, religion, gender, or region

2. University Stance
   KR Mangalam University maintains a ZERO TOLERANCE policy towards ragging in any form, in accordance with UGC Regulations on Curbing the Menace of Ragging in Higher Educational Institutions, 2009, and AICTE guidelines.

3. Punishments for Ragging
   Depending on the severity of the offense, actions may include:
   - Warning and mandatory counseling (first minor offense)
   - Suspension from classes for 1 to 4 weeks
   - Withholding of results, scholarships, or fee concessions
   - Debarment from examinations
   - Expulsion from hostel
   - Rustication from the university
   - Filing of FIR with local police under applicable laws
   - Reporting to UGC/AICTE for permanent record

4. How to Report Ragging
   - National Anti-Ragging Helpline: 1800-180-5522 (24/7, toll-free)
   - University Anti-Ragging Committee Email: (contact Dean of Students office)
   - Anonymous complaint submission available
   - Online complaint form on the university website under Student Grievances
   - Students can also report directly to their hostel warden or class mentor

5. Mandatory Declarations at Admission
   - Every student MUST sign an anti-ragging affidavit at the time of admission
   - Parents/guardians must also sign a declaration
   - Affidavits are to be submitted online at antiragging.in as per UGC/AICTE norms
   - Failure to submit the affidavit may result in denial of admission

6. Anti-Ragging Committee
   The university has an active Anti-Ragging Committee comprising:
   - Senior faculty members
   - Student representatives
   - Parent representatives
   - The committee investigates all complaints and takes action within 7 working days
   - The identity of the complainant is kept strictly confidential

7. Awareness and Prevention Programs
   - Mandatory anti-ragging orientation conducted during Freshers' Week
   - Mentorship program: Each fresher is assigned a senior student mentor
   - Monthly awareness sessions conducted by the counseling cell
   - Posters and awareness campaigns displayed across campus
   - Peer support groups for students facing any form of harassment
